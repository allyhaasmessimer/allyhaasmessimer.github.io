Readit

Readit is an application that leverages the capabilities of a third-party API, specifically the Google Books API. Users visiting the site can effortlessly explore a wide variety of books using the extensive resources offered by the Google Books API. By creating an account and logging in, users unlock the ability to build two distinct lists: a wish list cataloging books they aspire to read, and a comprehensive record of books they have already read. This personalized environment allows users to manage their literary interests with ease and precision.

MySwolePal

MySwolePal is a comprehensive workout planner that offers a range of features to help fitness enthusiasts achieve their fitness goals. With MySwolePal, users can create an account to access its full functionality. Once logged in, users can view their saved workouts, add new exercises, and delete workouts or exercises as needed. To get started, users can search MySwolePal's library of exercises, which is filtered based on muscle group, difficulty level, and exercise type. This helps users find exercises that match their fitness goals. From here, users can add exercises directly to their workout, customize their routine, and make it more effective.


Skills: FastAPI · Redux.js · PostgreSQL · React.js · Docker

Auto Dealership

The auto dealership application is a tool designed to streamline the management of automobile dealerships. With this app, dealership owners and managers can easily manage their inventory, service center, and sales processes in one centralized location. The app consists of three microservices, namely Sales, Inventory, and Services, which are able to interact seamlessly through polling. The backend is built using Django and is containerized using Docker, while the front-end is built using React. Overall, this app is scalable, flexible, and efficient, making it an ideal solution for managing the various aspects of automobile dealerships.


Skills: React.js · Docker · Python

About me

I am a Cloud Engineer with a unique background in biotechnology and software engineering. After earning a B.S. in biotechnology, I began my career as a lab technician, purifying allergens in a production environment for sale. Upon relocating to a small town with limited biotech opportunities, I checked out a JavaScript book at the local library and started coding.

As I learned more about coding and explored the possibilities of automation through scripts, I found myself drawing parallels to the excitement I felt in biotech, where applying biological principles advanced technological progress. I brought skills from biotech—such as experimental problem-solving, thorough documentation, and an exploratory mindset—into software development. Transitioning into software engineering, I focused on building and maintaining web applications using Python, JavaScript, React, Docker, Heroku, and SQL.

Recently, I completed a course on the fundamentals of cloud computing. I obtained certifications in AWS Cloud Practitioner, Microsoft Azure Fundamentals, and CompTIA Cloud+. Currently, I am expanding my cloud computing knowledge by concentrating on building cloud infrastructure with tools such as Terraform and Docker.
